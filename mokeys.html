ANIMALS MAP PLACES

Monkeys

monkey, in general, any of nearly 200 species of tailed primate, with the exception of lemurs, tarsiers, and lorises.

The presence of a tail (even if only a tiny nub), along with their narrow-chested bodies and other features of the skeleton, distinguishes monkeys from apes.

Most monkeys have a short, relatively flat face without great prominence of the muzzle, although baboons and mandrills are notable exceptions.

The vast majority of species live in tropical forests, where they move on all four limbs.
